You are classifying whether an emergency-call description meets ESI Level 2 (high-risk).

Context:
This step is ONLY evaluated if the patient does NOT meet ESI Level 1 criteria.

Task:
Determine whether the patient should be classified as ESI 2.

Definition:
ESI 2 applies to patients who are high-risk, likely to deteriorate, or require time-sensitive intervention, but who do NOT currently require immediate life-saving intervention.

Core decision questions:
- Is this a high-risk situation?
- Is the patient likely to deteriorate?
- Is there an acute change in mental status?
- Is the patient in severe pain or distress?

Classify as esi_2 = true if ANY of the following are present:

HIGH-RISK / TIME-SENSITIVE CONDITIONS:
- Symptoms suggestive of serious conditions requiring rapid treatment (e.g., acute coronary syndrome, stroke, ectopic pregnancy, sepsis risk)
- Immunocompromised patient with fever (e.g., chemotherapy, transplant)
- Active suicidal, homicidal, or violent behavior
- Sexual assault or severe psychological trauma
- Toxic ingestion or unclear overdose
- Significant trauma mechanism (e.g., high fall, ejection, penetrating injury without instability)

LIKELY TO DETERIORATE:
- Abnormal vital signs suggesting risk (tachycardia, tachypnea, hypoxia, hypotension without collapse)
- Increasing respiratory effort or moderate respiratory distress (but not failure)
- Cardiac complaints with concerning features (e.g., chest pain with abnormal ECG or high suspicion)
- Signs of infection with systemic risk (possible sepsis without shock)

ACUTE MENTAL STATUS CHANGE:
- New confusion, lethargy, disorientation, agitation, or altered mental status of unknown cause

SEVERE PAIN OR DISTRESS:
- Severe pain (typically ≥ 7/10) suggesting systemic or serious pathology (e.g., renal colic, sickle cell crisis, cancer pain)
- Severe physiological or psychological distress (e.g., combativeness, acute grief, panic with instability)

IMPORTANT EXCLUSIONS:
Do NOT classify as ESI 2 if:
- The patient clearly meets ESI 1 (requires immediate life-saving intervention)
- The patient is stable, low-risk, or symptoms are mild without risk of deterioration
- Pain is severe but due to minor, localized, non-systemic causes (e.g., isolated orthopedic injury without complications)

Ambiguity rule:
- If high-risk or deterioration is clearly suggested → return true
- Otherwise → return false
- Do not over-triage based on vague concern alone

Output rules:
- Respond using the provided schema
- justification must be concise and reference specific elements from the description
