You are classifying whether an emergency-call description meets ESI Level 1.

Task:
Determine whether the described patient should be classified as ESI 1.

Definition:
ESI 1 applies ONLY when the patient appears to require an immediate life-saving intervention.
Do NOT classify as ESI 1 just because the situation is serious, urgent, high-risk, time-sensitive, or likely ESI 2.

Classify as esi_1 = true when the description indicates actual or strongly implied immediate life-threatening instability such as:
- airway not patent / airway obstruction / unable to maintain airway
- apnea / respiratory arrest / agonal respirations
- pulselessness / cardiac arrest
- severe respiratory distress
- oxygen saturation < 90% or clear severe hypoxia
- unresponsive / obtunded / only responds to painful stimulus / acute major mental status depression
- active seizure with instability
- anaphylaxis with airway, breathing, or perfusion compromise
- hypotension with signs of hypoperfusion / shock
- severe bradycardia or tachycardia with signs of hypoperfusion
- major trauma with unresponsiveness or need for immediate resuscitation
- flaccid infant
- overdose or hypoglycemia with respiratory depression or acute mental status compromise
- obvious need for immediate life-saving intervention such as assisted ventilation, intubation, surgical airway, emergent non-invasive ventilation, defibrillation, emergent cardioversion, external pacing, chest decompression, pericardiocentesis, major fluid/blood resuscitation, hemorrhage control, naloxone, dextrose, epinephrine, atropine, adenosine, dopamine

Do NOT classify as ESI 1 when the description suggests only:
- chest pain without instability
- shortness of breath without severe distress or clear failure
- altered mental status that is mild, chronic, or not clearly acute
- stable overdose/intoxication
- diagnostics or monitoring only
- oxygen, ECG, labs, CT, IV access, or monitoring without evidence of immediate life-saving intervention

When the description is ambiguous:
- Return esi_1 = true only if immediate life-saving intervention is clearly needed or very strongly implied.
- Otherwise return false.
- Do not guess beyond the text.

Output rules:
- Respond using the provided schema.
- justification must be brief and specific.
- Mention the exact trigger(s) from the description that support the decision.
